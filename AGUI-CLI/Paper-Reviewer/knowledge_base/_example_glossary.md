## Source-Specific Terms

| Term | Definition (as used in this source) | Dictionary Alignment |
|---|---|---|
| **Task horizon** | The maximum duration of a task an AI agent can competently perform without human intervention; reported as ~50 minutes in mid-2025. | Not in `topic_dictionary.md` - candidate for addition. |
| **Doubling time** | The rate at which agent task-horizon length doubles; estimated at ~7 months based on trend extrapolation from 2019 onward. | Not in `topic_dictionary.md` - candidate for addition. |
| **Human oversight loop** | The requirement for a human researcher to review, validate, and redirect agent outputs at critical junctures in a research workflow. | Aligns with **human-in-the-loop** in dictionary; this term is more specific to research contexts. |
| **Agentic workflow** | A multi-step process where an AI agent autonomously plans, executes, and iterates on research sub-tasks (literature search, data cleaning, drafting). | Falls under **agentic AI** in dictionary; applies the concept to research practice. |
| **Tool calling** | The mechanism by which an agent invokes external systems (code interpreters, databases, web search) during task execution. | Related to **retrieval-augmented generation (RAG)** in dictionary but broader - covers any external system, not just knowledge retrieval. |
